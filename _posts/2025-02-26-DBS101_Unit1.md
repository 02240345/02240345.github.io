# Unit 1: Database Systems Fundamentals

Before starting this course, my understanding of databases was pretty basic. I thought of them as just Excel spreadsheets on steroids—a place where you store data and retrieve it when needed. I had heard of terms like SQL, MySQL, and DBMS, but honestly, I didn’t fully grasp how they all fit together.

Now that I’ve completed Unit 1, my perspective has completely changed. Databases are not just about storing data; they are about organizing, securing, and managing information efficiently. They form the backbone of modern technology, from banking systems to social media platforms.

## What I Learned in Unit 1

![DBS](/assets/images/DBS.png)

**Databases Are Everywhere**

One of the first things that struck me was how integrated databases are in everyday life. From Netflix recommendations to online banking transactions, databases work behind the scenes to make things run smoothly.

We also discussed real-world applications—how universities use databases for student records, hospitals for patient management, and businesses for inventory tracking. This made me realize just how powerful databases are.

**Why We Need Database Systems**

Before this course, I never thought about why we need databases. But when we compared them to traditional file storage (Excel, physical records, etc.), it became clear why databases are the better choice.

Here’s what I found interesting:

***File-based systems are messy:*** duplicate data, security risks, and difficulty in accessing information.

Databases solve these problems by ensuring data integrity, security, and efficiency.

**The Evolution of Databases**
I was surprised to learn that databases weren’t always as advanced as they are today.
Early databases were hierarchical, storing data in a tree-like structure.

Then came network databases, which introduced more complex relationships.

Finally, relational databases (RDBMS) like MySQL and PostgreSQL became the gold standard.
This historical perspective helped me understand why SQL and relational databases are so important today.
